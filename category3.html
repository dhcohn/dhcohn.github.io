The Name-games video projects, all of which were made with a cheap video camera and editing software, aim to loosely narrate the general structure of narrative itself—they simulate the exponential curve of iterative accumulation rather than the linear time of a story.

In “Self-Image, psychoanalytic themes combined with existential imagery take the viewer through a process of growing up. The mirror functions according to Lacan's mirror stage: that is, it is the medium through which an otherwise psychotic frenzy of pure difference is coalesced into a hierarchical self that distinguishes between itself and the world through the ubiquitous distinction of subject and object. “In Passing” is conversely a quasi-narrative of the essence of endings. As language provides the binding force that holds a lifetime together, that keeps time in a relation to itself, text is employed in this piece to allude to that kind of cohesion even while it falls apart. The title here refers both to those moments between the lines of narrative structure and to death as a kind of paradoxical drawn out in-between-time. Direct references to religion and the concept of God in the piece are meant to constitute the subject's relationship to death, but to a death that is perpetually outside —rather than simply after— the realm of life with all of its many words and names.
